What is climate change?

Climate change is defined as the change in usual weather of a place or region.
This is caused by global warming due to human activity and disregard for the planet earth.
If a combined global effort cannot be made to combat climate change and
limit the global temperature increase by about 1.5 degrees celsius there will be irreversable damage

What could happen if we dont act now?

  • The UK and Europe will be vulnerable to flooding caused by extreme rainfall
  • Countries in the Middle East will experience extreme heatwaves and farmland could turn to desert
  • Island nations in the Pacific region could disappear under rising seas
  • Many African nations are likely to suffer droughts and food shortages
  • Drought conditions are likely in the western US, while other areas will see more intense storms
  • Australia is likely to suffer extremes of heat and drought
  • Increase in category 5 storms
  • Oceans are the largest reservoir of CO2. The warmer it gets, the less CO2 can diffuse into an ocean and so more will remain in the atmosphere, which creates a positive feedback loop causing more warming
  • Only 3% of all water is freshwater and this percentage will reduce as ice caps melt
  • Mass extinctions could occur in the future
